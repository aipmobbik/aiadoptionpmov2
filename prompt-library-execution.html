Presentation Slide Generation
Transform project information into management-ready reports
Specific Heavy context Claude
▼

Input

  • Project brief or scope document
  • Presentation structure template (if available)
  • Key messages or talking points
💡 Define the audience and purpose upfront — AI tailors language, depth, and tone accordingly.

Output

  • Slide-by-slide content (title + 3–5 bullets per slide)
  • Speaker notes for each slide
  • Suggested visual elements or charts per slide

When to use

When creating a client or stakeholder presentation. Attach project brief + structure template.

Template
You are a [role, e.g., PMO Consultant / Presentation Designer]. Create a [N]-slide presentation for [Project Name] for [audience] on [topic/purpose] using the attached BBIK PowerPoint template as the primary design and formatting reference. Attach: [project brief + structure template + key talking points]. Audience: [executives / client / internal team]. Tone: [formal / persuasive / informative]. Max [N] bullets per slide. Output: 6 slides with title + bullets + speaker notes + suggested visual Evaluation: Each slide must have one clear message. Flag slides with >5 bullets or missing speaker notes.
Full example
You are a PMO Consultant creating a client-facing presentation for the Super App project. Create a 6-slide progress presentation for the ABC Corporation client on status (Week 22) using the attached BBIK PowerPoint template as the primary design and formatting reference. Attach: the project brief (scope: Digital Payments module) and the BBIK presentation template. Audience: Client C-Suite. Formal, reassuring tone. Max 5 bullets per slide. Output: 6 slides with title + bullets + speaker notes + suggested visual Evaluation: Each slide must have one clear message. Flag slides with >5 bullets or missing speaker notes.

Action Item Prioritization
Help PMOs focus on the most critical actions and proactively manage blockers
Specific Heavy context ChatGPT · Claude
▼

Input

  • Action tracker (all open items with IDs)
  • Jira export or task board snapshot
  • Latest project updates or status report
💡 Include due dates and stakeholder names — AI surfaces overdue high-impact items automatically.

Output

  • Prioritized action list (Critical / High / Medium / Low)
  • Overdue items flagged with escalation recommendation
  • This-week focus list (top 5 actions)

When to use

Weekly or before sprint planning. Attach action tracker + project updates.

Template
You are a [role, e.g., PMO Assistant]. Review the attached action tracker for [Project Name] (Week [X]). Prioritize all [N] open actions. Attach: [action tracker + Jira export or task board + latest project updates]. Prioritize by: due date, impact on project delivery, blocking status. Concise, actionable tone. Output: Prioritized list: Priority | Action ID | Description | Owner | Due Date | Status Overdue items with escalation recommendation This-week focus: Top 5 actions to complete Evaluation: Confirm all CRITICAL items have a named owner. Flag overdue actions with no escalation plan.
Full example
You are a PMO Assistant supporting the Super App project team. Review the action tracker for the Super App project (Week 18, 34 open actions). Prioritize all items. Attach: the action tracker, Sprint 4 Jira export, and the Week 18 status report. Prioritize by due date, delivery impact, and blocking status. Concise, actionable tone. Output: Prioritized list: Priority | Action ID | Description | Owner | Due Date | Status Overdue items with escalation recommendation This-week focus: Top 5 actions to complete Evaluation: Confirm all CRITICAL items have a named owner. Flag overdue actions with no escalation plan.

Initial Meeting Request Email
Craft professional, context-aware meeting emails in minutes
Generic Light context ChatGPT · Claude · Gemini · Copilot
▼

Input

  • Meeting purpose & key objectives
  • Attendee names, roles & organization
  • 2–3 proposed time slots & platform (Teams/Zoom/in-person)
💡 Provide actual date/time options with timezone — "next week" produces generic outputs.

Output

  • Professional meeting request email (ready to send)
  • Clear agenda aligned to objectives
  • Polite, formal tone — ready to send

When to use

When scheduling a first-time or formal meeting. No documents needed — works with text input.

Template
You are a [role, e.g., PMO Communication Lead]. Draft a formal meeting request email to [recipient name & role] at [organization] for [meeting purpose]. Objectives: [list 2–3 objectives]. Proposed slots: [option 1], [option 2], [option 3]. Platform: [Teams/Zoom/in-person]. [Formal] tone. Duration: [X mins]. Include a brief agenda. Output: Complete email with subject line, body, and proposed agenda. Evaluation: Confirm subject line is specific, agenda items match objectives, and tone is appropriate for [recipient level].
Full example
You are a professional PMO Communication Lead at Bluebik Group. Draft a formal meeting request email to the Head of Finance and IT Director at ABC Corporation for the ERP Integration project kickoff. Objectives: align on project scope, confirm resource commitments, agree on governance structure. Slots: Mon 23 Jun 10:00–11:00 BKK / Tue 24 Jun 14:00–15:00 / Wed 25 Jun 09:00–10:00. Platform: Microsoft Teams. Formal, professional tone. Duration: 60 mins. Include brief agenda. Output: Complete email with subject line, body, and proposed agenda. Evaluation: Subject line must name the project. Agenda must cover all 3 objectives. Tone appropriate for C-Suite.

Meeting Reschedule / Follow-up Email
Craft professional, context-aware meeting emails in minutes
Generic Light context ChatGPT · Claude · Gemini · Copilot
▼

Input

  • Original meeting title & date
  • Reason for rescheduling (optional)
  • 2–3 new proposed time slots
💡 Mention the original date in the email — it helps recipients recall the context immediately.

Output

  • Polite reschedule or follow-up email (ready to send)
  • Updated time slot options clearly presented
  • Professional tone maintaining relationship

When to use

When rescheduling a meeting or following up after no response. Paste context — no documents needed.

Template
You are a [role, e.g., PMO Coordinator]. Draft a [reschedule / follow-up] email for the [meeting title] originally scheduled on [original date]. Context: [brief reason for change, if applicable]. New slots: [option 1], [option 2], [option 3]. Polite, professional tone. Keep it brief — max 3 paragraphs. Output: Complete email with subject line and body. Evaluation: Confirm the original meeting is referenced, new slots are clearly stated, and tone is appropriate.
Full example
You are a PMO Coordinator at Bluebik Group. Draft a reschedule email for the ERP Integration Steering Committee originally planned for Week 18 (Mon 10 Jun) — postponed due to CFO travel. New proposed slots: Wed 19 Jun 14:00–15:30, Thu 20 Jun 10:00–11:30, Mon 24 Jun 14:00–15:30. Same attendees. Polite, professional tone. Reference original date. Max 3 paragraphs. Output: Complete email with subject line and body. Evaluation: Confirm original date referenced, all 3 new slots listed, and CFO travel not detailed (out of respect).

Draft MoM Distribution Email
Convert raw meeting notes into structured, actionable Minutes of Meeting
Generic Light context ChatGPT · Claude · Gemini · NotebookLM
▼

Input

  • Finalized MoM document
  • Attendee list (or reply-to email thread)
  • Meeting recording (Suggested for NotebookLM)
💡 Mention the key decisions in the email body — not everyone reads the full MoM attachment.

Output

  • Professional MoM distribution email
  • Key decisions and action items highlighted in the body
  • Ready to send with MoM as attachment

When to use

After MoM is ready. Paste the MoM — no other documents needed.

Template
You are a [role, e.g., PMO Communication Specialist]. Draft a distribution email to send the finalized MoM for [meeting name] on [date] to all attendees. Attach: [finalized MoM document + attendee list]. Include 2–3 key decisions and top action items in the email body. [Professional] tone. Keep under 150 words. Output: Complete email with subject line and body (MoM as attachment reference). Evaluation: Confirm key decisions are mentioned in the body. Flag if email exceeds 150 words.
Full example
You are a PMO Communication Specialist at Bluebik Group. Draft a distribution email to send the finalized MoM for Daily Meeting on 19 May 2025 to all attendees. Attach: the finalized MoM. Highlight 3 key decisions and top action items in the body. Professional tone. Under 150 words. Output: Complete email with subject line and body. Evaluation: 3 decisions must appear in body. Email must be ≤150 words. Tone appropriate for internal team.
